Independent creator completes 17-minute animated series solo using AI-assisted production workflow

June 2026

At a time when producing an animated series typically requires dozens, if not hundreds, of people, Frank Houbre took on an unusual challenge: producing a complete episode of an original animated series entirely on his own.

The result is Lost Garden, a dark fantasy series set in a vast underground world populated by knights, mysterious children, and forgotten creatures.

Released in early June, the first episode has already surpassed 65,000 views in just a few days, drawing enthusiastic comments from viewers impressed by the visual quality, art direction, and ambition of the project.

Over several weeks, Frank Houbre handled the entire production solo: writing, art direction, storyboard, animation, editing, sound design, music, and post-production.

Contrary to some assumptions, the project was not created by pressing a single button. The story, characters, world, narrative structure, and artistic choices were conceived and directed by its creator. Artificial intelligence served as a production accelerator, enabling an independent creator to realize a vision that would previously have required a full studio.

Beyond Lost Garden, Frank Houbre is also developing ScreenWeaver, software for screenwriters and filmmakers that bridges scriptwriting and AI-assisted storyboard visualization.

This approach reflects a broader shift in the audiovisual industry, where AI tools are gradually becoming creative assistants in service of writers, directors, and studios.

"My goal is not to replace artists or studios. On the contrary. I believe these tools will enable a new generation of creators to tell stories they could never have afforded to produce before."
— Frank Houbre
